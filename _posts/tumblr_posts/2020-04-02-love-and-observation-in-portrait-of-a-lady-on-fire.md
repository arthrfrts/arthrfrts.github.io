[Love And Observation In PORTRAIT OF A LADY ON FIRE](https://t.umblr.com/redirect?z=https%3A%2F%2Fbirthmoviesdeath.com%2F2020%2F02%2F24%2Flove-and-observation-in-portrait-of-a-lady-on-fire&t=NWNhNDI0MGJlNzAzZWMxODllYzkwMWQxMTQ0MDBlZTU4ZjQ3ODM3OCw4ZjBmNzQ0ODEwN2NkZWI3NTdmMDM4NjI4MWMwYzZkMjk1N2U1YTBh)

> Observation is the first step in creation – in building a work of art – and the film reflects this in its empty stretches of space and sound, especially at the beginning. The music is minimal, so when we hear it, it always carries emotional weight. The images, as well, build as the film does, crowding the frame with more characters at a time. Observation is also the first step to falling in love, but it cannot be complete until it is returned.

> A lot of films focus on the fact that, when you make art about a person, it creates a facsimile of them – a shadow that can never really measure up to the person themselves.
